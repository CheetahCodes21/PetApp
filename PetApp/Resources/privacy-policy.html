1Information we collect

Account information

Name, email address, and any profile details you provide during onboarding, handled through our authentication and database provider (Supabase).

Journal content

Voice recordings, transcribed text, photos, and any notes you add to your journal entries. This may include sensitive personal reflections, memories, or details about your life and relationships. Journal content is currently stored on your device.

Companion data

Your chosen companion type (e.g., dog, cat, plant), feeding activity, and interaction history within the app.

Usage data

App interactions, feature usage, reminder preferences, and general device/technical information (device type, operating system, app version).

Calendar metadata

Dates and timestamps associated with saved journal entries, used to power the Memory calendar view.

Permissions

With your consent, we access your microphone (to record memories), camera (to take a photo for a memory), photo library (to attach existing photos), and notifications (for reminders). You can grant or revoke each of these individually in your device settings at any time.

2How we use your information

We use your information to:

  • Provide core app functionality (voice-to-text transcription, journal storage, calendar search)
  • Personalize your companion experience and reminder frequency
  • Maintain and improve app performance and reliability
  • Send reminders and prompts you've opted into — as on-device notifications, not through a push notification server
  • Provide customer support

We do not sell your personal information or journal content to third parties.

3Voice data and transcription

Your voice recordings are transcribed to text using Apple's speech recognition. Whenever your device supports it, this happens entirely on your device and the audio is not sent anywhere. When on-device transcription isn't available — for example on some languages or older devices — the audio is sent securely to Apple's speech recognition service to generate the transcript, and to no other third party. This processing is subject to Apple's Privacy Policy; we do not receive or retain a copy of the audio on our own servers. Original audio recordings are stored on your device so you can play them back later, alongside their transcripts and any photos you attach. We show you this explanation the first time you record a memory, and it's available any time from Settings.

4How we share information

We may share information with:

  • Service providers who help us operate the app — currently Supabase, which handles account sign-in and basic account records under our Row-Level Security rules
  • Legal authorities, if required by law or to protect rights, safety, or property

We do not share your journal content with other users or make it public, and we do not use any advertising or analytics tools that collect your data.

5Data storage and security

Your account details are stored securely with Supabase, our backend provider. Your journal entries, recordings, and photos are currently stored on your device. Access to your content is restricted to what's needed to provide the service. No method of storage or transmission is 100% secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

It started with a question.

How do we hold onto the moments that matter before they quietly fade?

Where the idea came from

MemoMe began during the early research phase of our Apple Foundation Project. As we spoke with older adults, family members of older adults, and professionals who work closely with ageing communities, one idea kept coming up: memory rarely disappears all at once. Instead, it changes gradually. Sometimes it becomes harder to remember what happened yesterday, where the keys were left, or how a story began. Yet a familiar photo, a favourite meal, or the right question can bring a memory back in an instant.

A gentle invitation, not something lost

That idea stayed with us. We started thinking about memory not as something that's simply lost, but as something that often needs a gentle invitation to return. Research on ageing suggests that while recalling recent events can become more challenging over time, meaningful cues—such as familiar places, objects, conversations, and emotions—can help unlock memories that feel just out of reach. We wondered how those moments of recall could become part of an everyday habit rather than something that only happens by chance.

Starting with something smaller

As we continued exploring, we realised another challenge. Keeping a journal is a wonderful idea, but staring at a blank page can feel overwhelming. The hardest part is often knowing where to begin. So we started with something smaller. Instead of asking someone to write about their entire life, MemoMe begins with simple prompts inspired by everyday experiences. A meal you loved. A place you visited often. Someone who made you laugh. The little moments that are easy to talk about, yet meaningful enough to lead to bigger stories. One memory naturally leads to another, making reflection feel less like a task and more like a conversation.

A companion, not just an app

We also wanted people to have a reason to come back—not because they had to, but because they wanted to. That's why we created a small companion that grows alongside your memories. Looking after it becomes a gentle daily ritual, and each visit is another opportunity to capture a story, revisit a memory, or rediscover one you hadn't thought about in years.

A little easier to find, a little harder to lose

Although MemoMe was inspired by conversations around healthy ageing, we've come to believe that its purpose reaches far beyond one age group. Everyone has moments worth remembering. Everyone has stories that deserve a place to stay. MemoMe is our way of making those stories a little easier to find, a little more enjoyable to revisit, and a little harder to lose.
